Logo Concepts — 4 Directions
Concept A
Speed / Velocity
Speed Mark
Motion lines lead into a sharp V — communicates speed, momentum, and forward progress. The most literal expression of the "Velo" name (from velocity).
Best for: Conveying energy and pace of delivery. Appeals to founders who want to move fast.
Concept B
AI Intelligence
Intelligence Node
A central node radiating connections — represents Velo as the intelligence hub that connects strategy, operations, marketing, and data across a client's business.
Best for: Emphasizing the AI/systems angle. Appeals to tech-forward clients and the consulting track.
Concept C
Trust / Authority
Trust Shield
A shield silhouette with the V inside — projects reliability, protection, and advisor credibility. Positions Velo as the trusted partner guarding your business.
Best for: Building trust with risk-averse small business owners. Conveys stability and professionalism.
Concept D
Modern / Minimal
Minimal V
A single bold stroke forming the V — nothing extra. Maximum clarity, maximum confidence. The logo that gets out of the way and lets the work speak.
Best for: Versatility across all sizes. Works equally well as a favicon, LinkedIn avatar, or business card. Easiest to reproduce.

Design Rationale

Why four directions: Each concept emphasizes a different facet of the Velo brand — speed of delivery, intelligence of the system, trustworthiness as an advisor, or clean modern professionalism. Testing all four helps identify which emotional register resonates most with Hayden and with the target market.

Color integration: All concepts use the established Velo Brand Color Palette — Velo Amber (#E07A2F) as the primary mark color, Deep Night (#1C1C2A) for dark backgrounds, and Warm White (#FBF7F2) for light contexts. The amber "V" accent creates a consistent thread across all four directions.

Wordmark treatment: The wordmark uses a clean sans-serif with tight letter-spacing and a subtle amber accent on the "o" — this works independently of whichever logo mark is selected, giving flexibility for lockup configurations (mark + wordmark, wordmark only, mark only).

Next step: Hayden selects a direction (or hybrid), then we refine into final lockups for LinkedIn, dashboard header, business card, and favicon.
